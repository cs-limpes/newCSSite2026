About

Systems work, human experience, and the places where they meet.

Charlene Slimp is a technology, marketing, and business systems consultant with a background in software development, accessibility, digital strategy, infrastructure, training, and small business operations.

Her work has often lived in the messy middle: the place where a website problem turns out to be a business process problem, a marketing problem turns out to be a data problem, or a technical system is quietly shaping what people can understand, access, and do.

She helps small businesses, agencies, creators, and mission-driven teams make digital systems clearer, sturdier, more accessible, and more useful. This includes websites, WordPress, analytics, marketing workflows, accessibility, sponsor materials, operational processes, documentation, and practical technology decisions.

Current Academic Direction

Charlene is also pursuing further study in clinical counseling and psychology, with a focus on trauma, somatics, chronic illness, embodied experience, and psychedelic studies.

This direction grows out of a long-standing interest in how people survive, adapt, make meaning, and participate inside systems that were not always designed for their bodies, histories, capacities, or realities.

For a more current snapshot of how these threads are developing, see Current Focus.

Orientation

Charlene's approach is practical, interdisciplinary, and human-centered. She is interested in systems that work in real life, not just in theory: systems people can understand, maintain, question, and participate in without disappearing parts of themselves.

That orientation shows up in both her consulting work and her academic path. The common thread is attention to structure, context, access, agency, and lived experience.
