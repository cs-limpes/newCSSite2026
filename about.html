About

Interdisciplinary work on access, systems, technology, and lived experience.

Charlene Slimp is an interdisciplinary researcher and human systems consultant whose work sits between accessibility, technology, AI ethics, organizational systems, embodied experience, chronic illness, and human participation.

Her work is concerned with how people move through systems that were not always designed with their bodies, capacities, contexts, or agency in mind. She is especially interested in the places where technical systems, institutional habits, and human experience meet.

This site keeps a public record of current research interests, selected projects, writing, and professional materials. It is intended to support academic admissions, career networking, collaboration, and thoughtful inquiry.

Working Areas

  • Accessibility and inclusive participation
  • AI ethics, governance, and social consequences
  • Human systems, organizational behavior, and decision-making
  • Chronic illness, embodiment, and knowledge production
  • Research translation, facilitation, and systems consulting

Orientation

Charlene's approach is grounded in practical systems thinking, careful language, and respect for lived experience as a source of knowledge. The work is research-informed without treating people as abstractions, and applied without reducing complex human questions to simple productivity problems.
