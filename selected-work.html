Selected Work

Projects where technology, access, marketing, and business systems had to work together.

Overview

My work often begins with a system that is technically present but not functioning well enough for the people depending on it. I help identify what is broken, clarify what matters first, and build practical paths forward across technology, marketing, accessibility, and operations.

The examples below show selected consulting, software, accessibility, infrastructure, and strategy work across small businesses, agencies, enterprise teams, and creator/media projects.

Work Index

Infrastructure, WordPress, systems administration, client support

Patrick Hildreth Brand & Design

Managed and secured a multi-site WordPress environment for a design agency supporting 30+ client sites. Work included hosting and server troubleshooting, backups, updates, access control, staging workflows, DNS, SSL, email deliverability, plugin conflicts, custom WordPress functionality, analytics support, and client training.

Focused on stabilizing fragile systems, reducing avoidable downtime, and giving the agency a more reliable foundation for client launches, redesigns, and ongoing support.

Accessibility, enterprise software, design systems, training

Pattern

The common thread is systems translation.

Across these projects, the work has usually involved translating between people, platforms, processes, and goals: making technical systems understandable, making business decisions more grounded, and making digital environments easier to use, maintain, and improve.
