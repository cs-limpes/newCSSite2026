Where My Attention Is Now

My current work sits in a transitional but connected space. Professionally, I am consulting across technology, marketing, business systems, accessibility, and digital infrastructure. Academically, I am moving toward clinical counseling and psychology, with a focus on trauma, somatics, chronic illness, and psychedelic studies.

These paths may look separate from the outside, but they keep returning to the same questions: how systems shape people, how people adapt inside systems, and what it takes to make participation, repair, healing, and agency more possible.

Active Questions

How do people rebuild trust in their bodies, perceptions, and choices after trauma, illness, coercion, or prolonged stress?

What happens when chronic illness becomes a source of knowledge rather than only a clinical problem to manage?

How can somatic approaches support people whose nervous systems have been shaped by danger, instability, grief, medical systems, family systems, or social conditions?

What kinds of digital, institutional, and interpersonal systems make participation easier, and which ones quietly require people to disappear parts of themselves in order to function?

How can small businesses and creative projects build practical systems that support real human capacity instead of depending on burnout, improvisation, and invisible labor?

Near-Term Priorities

  • Build a clearer consulting practice around websites, marketing systems, accessibility, analytics, sponsorship infrastructure, and digital operations.
  • Continue academic and professional preparation in counseling psychology, trauma, somatics, chronic illness, and psychedelic studies.
  • Develop selected writing around embodiment, systems, access, healing, labor, and meaning-making.
  • Document selected consulting projects in a way that shows both the technical work and the human systems underneath it.
  • Maintain a public professional home that can hold consulting, research, writing, teaching, and future clinical work without forcing them into separate identities.

Working premise

Systems are never just technical.

The systems people use every day, websites, workflows, tools, institutions, stories, bodies, families, businesses, platforms, all shape what becomes easy, difficult, visible, hidden, possible, or forbidden. My current work is about understanding those systems and making better choices inside them.
